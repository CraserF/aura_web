Water Infrastructure · Investment Mechanisms
Mechanism 01
Blended Finance
Concessional capital from DFIs and climate funds is layered with commercial debt to absorb risk and reduce the cost of capital — making projects financeable that would otherwise be too risky for private lenders alone.
Mechanism 02
Credit Guarantee Vehicle
Targeted for South Africa in 2026. Provides partial guarantees for infrastructure projects, attracting private lenders by absorbing first-loss risk and improving bankability for municipalities.
SA 2026 Partial Guarantee First-loss layer
Mechanism 03
Blue Bonds
Debt instruments specifically for water and marine projects. Small municipalities — individually too small for the bond market — can be pooled into a single issuance, achieving the scale needed to attract institutional capital.
Mechanism 04
Ring-Fenced Utility Model
Moving toward autonomous water entities where tariff revenue is protected and reinvested exclusively into water infrastructure — breaking the cycle of cross-subsidisation.
Green Climate Fund — SA Water Reuse Programme
Secured funding to accelerate water reuse infrastructure across qualifying South African municipalities, reducing reliance on freshwater abstraction and building climate resilience.
