Ship enterprise search as a focused workflow layer

The strongest path is a narrow launch around source-backed answers, governed connectors, and measurable support deflection rather than a broad knowledge portal.

Decision

Recommendation

Approve a 90-day launch focused on three governed workflows: account research, policy lookup, and support answer drafting.

DecisionFund the focused workflow layer.

The narrow scope gives teams a visible quality bar while avoiding a long platform build before users see value.

Context

Why this matters now

Teams already search across CRM notes, support history, and internal policy pages, but answers are inconsistent and hard to verify. The launch should solve the highest-frequency workflows first.

The product risk is not discovery; it is trusted synthesis with provenance.

Recommendation

Implementation posture

Keep the first release small enough that quality, ownership, and measurement stay legible.

  1. 01

    Constrain the workflow set

    Launch three repeatable workflows with known source systems and user owners.

  2. 02

    Make provenance visible

    Every answer must cite source records, policy pages, or support cases before it can be shared.

  3. 03

    Measure resolution quality

    Track accepted answers, escalation avoidance, and correction reasons from the first pilot week.

Risks

Risks and controls

The main risks are quality drift, unclear ownership, and scope expansion before the quality bar is proven.

Answer trust

Require citations, correction capture, and human review for low-confidence responses.

Control required

Connector sprawl

Start with approved systems only, then add connectors when a workflow owner is named.

Limit scope

Metric ambiguity

Define accepted answer and avoided escalation before the pilot begins.

Resolve before launch
